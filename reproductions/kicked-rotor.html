Numerical Reproduction of Rozenbaum, Ganeshan & Galitski

Phys. Rev. Lett. 118, 086801 (2017) · May 2026

Abstract

We present a complete numerical reproduction of Rozenbaum, Ganeshan & Galitski (2017), which established that the out-of-time-ordered correlator (OTOC) growth rate (CGR) is fundamentally distinct from the classical Lyapunov exponent (LE) in the kicked rotor. All five figures of the original paper are reproduced with full quantum Floquet-operator evolution (Figs. 1–3), classical tangent-map methods (Figs. 2, 5), and long-time quantum dynamics in the localized regime (Fig. 4).

Physical Model

Kicked Rotor Hamiltonian

The dimensionless Hamiltonian of the quantum kicked rotor (QKR) is \[ \hat{H} = \frac{\hat{p}^{2}}{2} + K\cos(\hat{x})\,\Delta(t),\qquad \Delta(t)=\sum_{j=-\infty}^{\infty}\delta(t-j), \] where \(\hat{p}=-i\hbar_{\rm eff}\,\partial/\partial\hat{x}\) and \(\hat{x}\in[0,2\pi)\). Two parameters control the physics: the kicking strength \(K\) and the dimensionless effective Planck constant \(\hbar_{\rm eff}\).

Classical Standard Map

Integrating Hamilton's equations over one kick yields the Chirikov–Taylor standard map: \[ p_{n+1} = p_n + K\sin x_n,\qquad x_{n+1} = x_n + p_{n+1} \pmod{2\pi}. \] At \(K \lesssim K_{\rm cr}\approx 0.97\) the dynamics are predominantly regular (KAM tori intact); above this threshold global chaos emerges.

Quantum Floquet Operator

Stroboscopic evolution over one kick is governed by \[ \hat{F}=e^{-i\hat{p}^{2}/2\hbar_{\rm eff}}\;e^{-iK\cos(\hat{x})/\hbar_{\rm eff}}, \] applied in split-step: the kinetic part is diagonal in momentum basis \(\{|n\rangle\}\), the potential part is diagonal in position basis via DFT. The initial state is a Gaussian wave packet in momentum space with \(p_0=0\) and \(\sigma=4\).

Key Quantities

OTOC and Two-Point Correlator

\[ C(t)=-\big\langle\!\big[\hat{p}(t),\hat{p}(0)\big]^{2}\big\rangle,\qquad B(t)={\rm Re}\,\big\langle\hat{p}(t)\,\hat{p}(0)\big\rangle, \] where \(\hat{p}(t)=\hat{F}^{-t}\hat{p}\,\hat{F}^{t}\) is the Heisenberg-picture momentum operator.

Classical Lyapunov Exponent

\[ \lambda=\bigg\langle\!\!\!\bigg\langle\; \lim_{t\to\infty}\lim_{d(0)\to 0}\frac{1}{t}\ln\frac{d(t)}{d(0)} \bigg\rangle\!\!\!\bigg\rangle, \] where \(d(t)=\sqrt{[\Delta x(t)]^{2}+[\Delta p(t)]^{2}}\). Numerically computed via the tangent map.

Classical OTOC and the CGR

The classical correspondence of the OTOC is \[ C_{\rm cl}(t)=\hbar_{\rm eff}^{2}\; \bigg\langle\!\!\!\bigg\langle\! \left(\frac{\Delta p(t)}{\Delta x(0)}\right)^{2}\! \bigg\rangle\!\!\!\bigg\rangle\sim e^{2\tilde{\lambda}t}. \] Crucially, \(\tilde{\lambda}\neq\lambda\) because of the different order of averaging and logarithm: \(\tilde{\lambda}\sim\ln\langle\cdot\rangle\) versus \(\lambda\sim\langle\ln(\cdot)\rangle\). Jensen's inequality guarantees \(\tilde{\lambda}>\lambda\) for all non-trivial \(K\).

Ehrenfest-Time Singularity (Fig. 3)

The logarithmic derivative \(d(t)\equiv[\ln C(t)-\ln C(t-1)]/2\) eliminates the \(\hbar_{\rm eff}^{2}\) prefactor. At \(t < t_{\rm E}\), \(d(t)\approx\tilde{\lambda}\) (flat plateau). At \(t = t_{\rm E}\sim|\ln\hbar_{\rm eff}|/\ln(K/2)\), quantum interference sets in, and \(d(t)\) decays as a power law.

Numerical Methods

Classical Computation

Standard map iteration with tangent map for Lyapunov exponent. Monte Carlo sampling (\(2\times10^{5}\) trajectories) for classical CGR, with the critical insight that \(\langle\eta^{2}(t)\rangle\) must be averaged over trajectories first, then the log-ratio taken — otherwise the Jensen-inequality error is introduced.

Quantum OTOC (Figs. 1–3)

Forward–backward Floquet evolution with adaptive basis size: \[ N = 2^{\lceil\log_{2}(\alpha\cdot\max(p_{\rm rms},\,2\sigma)/\hbar_{\rm eff})\rceil}, \] with safety factor \(\alpha=2.5\)–\(4\). For \(K=1000\) at \(\hbar_{\rm eff}=2^{-14}\), \(N=2^{26}\approx6.7\times10^{7}\). The \(\mathcal{O}(T^{2})\) cost arises from the nested loop over backward steps: \(\sum_{k=1}^{T}2(T-k+1)=T(T+1)\) backward Floquet applications.

Long-Time Correlator (Fig. 4)

At \(\hbar_{\rm eff}=1\) (dynamically localized regime), basis is \(N=16\) (32 states). Forward-only evolution, \(\mathcal{O}(\tau N\log N)=\mathcal{O}(\tau)\) cost. \(\tau=3\times10^{6}\) completes in \(\sim1\) s per \(K\) value.

Reproduced Figures

Fig. 1 — OTOC and Two-Point Correlator

\(\hbar_{\rm eff}=2^{-14}\), \(\sigma=4\), \(p_0=0\). Adaptive basis \(N\in[2^{19},2^{21}]\), 30 kicks, 5-ensemble average. Upper panel: \(C(t)\) (semilog). Lower panel: \(B(t)\) (linear). The EW \(\sim9\) min, \(\sim4\) GB memory.

Fig. 2 — CGR versus LE

Four curves: quantum CGR from Floquet-evolved \(C(t)\) at \(\hbar_{\rm eff}=2^{-14}\) (red circles, 29 \(K\) values); classical CGR via tangent-map MC (green solid, 80 \(K\)); classical LE via tangent-map grid (blue triangles); Chirikov formula \(\lambda\approx\ln(K/2)\) (black dashed). The CGR exceeds the LE for all \(K\), with difference largest near \(K_{\rm cr}\). Wall time \(\sim2\) min classical + \(\sim2\) min quantum.

Fig. 3 — Ehrenfest-Time Singularity

Logarithmic derivative \(d(t)\) vs. \(t\) (log-log). Flat plateau \(=\) CGR; downward slope \(=\) power-law regime. \(\hbar_{\rm eff}=2^{-14}\). \(K=3\) required 100 kicks (\(N=2^{20}\), \(\sim32\) min) to observe the full transition. \(K=10\) used 30-kick Fig. 1 data.

Fig. 4 — Long-Time Averaged Correlator

\(\bar{B}_{\tau}(K)\) at \(\hbar_{\rm eff}=1\) for \(\tau=3\times10^{4},3\times10^{5},3\times10^{6}\). Sharp step at \(K_{\rm cr}\approx0.97\) traces the classical regular-to-chaotic transition even deep in the quantum regime. Larger \(\tau\) sharpens the step. \(\sim80\) s for 3 \(\tau\) values.

Fig. 5 — Classical LE in Polar Representation

\(\lambda(x,p)\) at \(K=1\) (just above \(K_{\rm cr}\)) on a \(200\times200\) grid, \(p\in[-16,16]\), 400 kicks per point. Polar coordinates \((\theta=x, r=p+16)\) reveal the mixed phase space: regular KAM islands (\(\lambda\approx0\), blue) embedded in a chaotic sea (\(\lambda>0\), yellow). \(\sim3\) min.

Resource Assessment

Complete reproduction of all five figures requires approximately 45 minutes wall time (parallel execution), 50 GB peak memory (Fig. 2 large-\(K\) quantum tasks), and <100 MB disk. Parallelized over independent \(K\) values and ensemble members via multiprocessing.Pool, with near-linear speedup up to 192 CPU cores on the h100 node.
